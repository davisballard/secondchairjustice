# Bing (Microsoft Ads) Copy Best Practices

> **Purpose:** Insight hub for writing maximum-performance Bing/Microsoft Ads copy for Second Chair.
> 
> **Sources:** Microsoft Advertising documentation, search marketing best practices, and PPC practitioner insights (2025-2026).
> 
> **Critical Platform Insight:** Bing users skew older (35-65), more affluent, and are often in decision-making roles. Lower competition than Google typically means better CPL.
>
> **Compliance Layer:** All copy must pass through [`Third_Person_Rules.md`](../03_Voice/Third_Person_Rules.md), [`Banned_Phrases.md`](../03_Voice/Banned_Phrases.md), and [`Platform_Rules.md`](../05_Restrictions/Platform_Rules.md).
>
> **Workflow Note:** Microsoft Ads allows direct import from Google Ads. Build for Google first, then optimize for Bing's older audience.
>
> **Last Updated:** January 2026

# Section 1: RESPONSIVE SEARCH ADS - HEADLINES (Insight Hub)

Bing RSAs work similarly to Google RSAs, with up to 15 headlines.

---

## Specifications

| Attribute | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Headlines available** | 3-15 | Minimum 3 required |
| **Character limit** | 30 characters each | Same as Google |
| **Displayed at once** | Up to 3 | Algorithm selects |

---

## Bing Audience Considerations for Headlines

> **Key Insight:** Bing users are older and more professional. Messaging should reflect this.

### Audience Profile Impact on Copy

| Google Audience | Bing Audience | Copy Adjustment |
|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Broader age range | Skews 35-65 | More formal language acceptable |
| Mixed intent | Often research-focused | Educational angles work well |
| Mobile-heavy | Desktop-heavy | Can use longer, more detailed copy |
| Price-sensitive | Higher income | Quality/trust messaging > price messaging |

---

## Headlines for Older Demographics

### What Works for Bing's Audience

| Focus | Example Headlines | Characters |
|-------|-------------------|------------|
| **Trust/Experience** | "Experienced Injury Attorneys" | 28 |
| **Professionalism** | "Trusted Legal Professionals" | 27 |
| **No-Risk** | "No Fee Unless You Win" | 22 |
| **Clear Value** | "Free Case Evaluation Today" | 26 |
| **Authority** | "25+ Years of Experience" | 23 |
| **Process Clarity** | "Speak With an Attorney Now" | 26 |

### Second Chair Bing Headlines

| Headline | Characters | Angle |
|----------|------------|-------|
| "Car Accident Cases Accepted" | 28 | Case type + Status |
| "Speak With an Attorney Today" | 27 | Action + Timeline |
| "Free Case Evaluation" | 20 | Clear offer |
| "No Upfront Cost" | 15 | Risk reversal |
| "Legal Help Available Now" | 24 | Availability |
| "Experienced Injury Attorneys" | 28 | Authority |
| "Get Compensation Help" | 21 | Benefit |
| "Trusted Legal Team" | 18 | Trust signal |

---

## Headline Specific Tips for Bing

| Tip | Why | Evidence |
|-----|-----|----------|
| **More formal language works** | Older audience expectations | Professional tone resonates |
| **Trust signals matter more** | Older users more skeptical | "Trusted", "Experienced", "Established" |
| **Include phone numbers** | Desktop users can call easily | Click-to-call effective on Bing |
| **Less urgency, more reassurance** | Older users don't respond to pressure | "At your convenience" > "Act NOW" |
| **Same character limits as Google** | Import-friendly | Optimize after import |

# Section 2: RESPONSIVE SEARCH ADS - DESCRIPTIONS (Insight Hub)

Descriptions provide the detailed value proposition.

---

## Specifications

| Attribute | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Descriptions available** | 2-4 | Minimum 2 required |
| **Character limit** | 90 characters each | Same as Google |
| **Displayed at once** | Up to 2 | Algorithm selects |

---

## Description Strategy for Bing

### Bing-Specific Considerations

| Factor | Implication for Copy |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Older audience** | More detail appreciated |
| **Desktop-heavy** | Less truncation concern |
| **Higher income** | Quality over price messaging |
| **Research-focused** | Educational copy works |

---

## Second Chair Description Examples for Bing

| Description | Characters | Elements |
|-------------|------------|----------|
| "Experienced attorneys handling car accident cases. Free consultation, no upfront fees. Call today." | 90 | Authority + Offer + Risk Reversal + CTA |
| "Compensation may be available for qualifying accident cases. Speak with a licensed attorney." | 88 | Outcome + Qualifier + Authority |
| "Over 25 years helping accident victims. No fee unless we win your case. Free evaluation." | 85 | Experience + Risk Reversal + Offer |
| "Get the legal representation you deserve. Free case review with experienced injury attorneys." | 88 | Value + Offer + Authority |

---

## Description Specific Tips for Bing

| Tip | Why | Evidence |
|-----|-----|----------|
| **Include credibility markers** | Older audience values experience | "25 years", "thousands of cases" |
| **Mention phone availability** | Desktop users prefer calling | "Call now" works better on Bing |
| **Complete sentences** | Professional appearance | Fragments feel rushed to older users |
| **Clear process explanation** | Reduces uncertainty | "Free consultation, then we discuss options" |

## Long Headline Strategy (90 Characters)

The long headline gives more room for complete messaging.

| Long Headline Example | Characters |
|-----------------------|------------|
| "Were You Injured in a Car Accident? Free Case Evaluation With Experienced Attorneys." | 85 |
| "Get the Legal Help You Deserve After an Accident. No Fee Unless We Win. Call Today." | 83 |
| "Accident Victims May Be Entitled to Compensation. Free Consultation With Licensed Attorneys." | 90 |

---

## Display Ad Specific Tips

| Tip | Why |
|-----|-----|
| **Use full 90 characters** | More context = better understanding |
| **Match image messaging** | Visual and text should align |
| **Include CTA** | "Call today" or "Free consultation" |
| **Trust signals** | "Licensed", "Experienced", "Trusted" |

# Section 6: UNIVERSAL PRINCIPLES (Cross-Cutting Insights)

Techniques that apply across all Bing/Microsoft Ads formats.

---

## The Bing Audience Difference

> **Key Insight:** Don't just import Google ads unchanged. Optimize for Bing's older, more affluent audience.

### Tone Adjustments for Bing

| Google Tone | Bing Optimization |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Urgent | Reassuring |
| Brief | More detailed |
| Mobile-first | Desktop-friendly |
| Price-focused | Quality-focused |
| Casual | Professional |

---

## Trust-First Messaging

Older audiences require more trust signals before converting.

### Trust Elements to Include

| Element | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Experience** | "25+ years", "Established 1998" |
| **Credentials** | "Licensed attorneys", "Board certified" |
| **Social Proof** | "Thousands of clients", "A+ BBB rating" |
| **Risk Reversal** | "No fee unless we win" |
| **Transparency** | "Free consultation", "No obligation" |

---

## Call-Friendly Copy

Bing users are more likely to call than click.

### Optimizing for Phone Leads

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|---------------|
| **Include phone number** | Use call extensions |
| **Mention calling in copy** | "Call for free consultation" |
| **Emphasize availability** | "Attorneys available now" |
| **Desktop-friendly** | Longer descriptions work |

---

## Import + Optimize Strategy

### Step 1: Import from Google

- Campaigns
- Ad groups
- Keywords
- Ads

### Step 2: Optimize for Bing

| Optimization | Why |
|--------------|-----|
| Add trust language | Older audience needs it |
| Extend descriptions | Desktop users read more |
| Add call CTAs | Phone leads more common |
| Test professional tone | May outperform casual |

---

## Second Chair Compliance on Bing

### Same Rules as Google

- Third-person language required
- No guarantees or promises
- Clear about not being a law firm
- No injury imagery

### Third-Person Language Examples

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use |
|---------|--------|
| "Were you injured?" | "Accident cases being accepted" |
| "You deserve compensation" | "Compensation may be available" |
| "Call if you were hurt" | "Speak with an attorney today" |

---

## Testing Framework

### What to Test on Bing

| Variable | Test Variations |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Tone** | Professional vs. Approachable |
| **Trust signals** | More vs. Fewer |
| **CTA type** | "Call" vs. "Learn More" |
| **Description length** | Full 90 chars vs. Shorter |
| **Urgency level** | Soft vs. None |

### Bing-Specific Metrics

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Cost per lead** | Often lower than Google |
| **Phone conversions** | Higher on Bing |
| **Quality Score** | Same importance as Google |
| **Desktop vs. Mobile** | Desktop dominates on Bing |
