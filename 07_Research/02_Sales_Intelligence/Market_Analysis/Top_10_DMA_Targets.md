## Methodology

### Data Sources & Estimation Framework

**Market sizing** is based on:
- Nielsen DMA population rankings (2025)
- ABA lawyer demographics (attorney counts by metro)
- ATRA legal advertising reports (2024)
- Google Ads Keyword Planner CPC data for PI keywords by metro (Q4 2025)
- State court filing data (annual PI/tort civil filings)
- IBISWorld PI attorney industry report ($61.7B national revenue, 2024)

**CPL estimates** are derived from:
- Google Ads CPC for PI keywords ("car accident lawyer," "personal injury attorney," "truck accident lawyer") by metro
- Industry-standard conversion rates: 5-8% click-to-lead on search, 1-3% on social/display
- Second Chair's actual media costs across current active markets (CA, CO, WA)
- Blended CPL assumes multi-channel mix (paid search 40%, paid social 35%, programmatic/display 25%)

**Competition level** reflects:
- Number of active PI lead gen vendors operating in the market
- Google Ads auction density for PI keywords
- Presence of mega-firms (Morgan & Morgan, etc.) consuming organic demand

### Scoring Dimensions

Each DMA is evaluated on a 10-point scale across:

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Market Size | 25% | PI firm count, annual filings, population |
| CPL Efficiency | 20% | Second Chair margin at $400/lead vs. media cost |
| Competition Level | 15% | Lead gen vendor saturation, auction density |
| Average Case Values | 15% | Settlement/verdict ranges, insurance minimums |
| Strategic Proximity | 15% | Team location, travel ease, existing relationships |
| Ease of Acquisition | 10% | Buyer sophistication, existing lead gen adoption |

- **The I-5/I-15/I-8 corridor system** and San Diego's car-dependent suburban sprawl (Chula Vista, Escondido, Oceanside, El Cajon) generate steady MVA volume
- **Cross-border dynamics** — San Diego's proximity to the Mexican border creates a bilingual population and cross-border commuter traffic (San Ysidro is the busiest land border crossing in the Western Hemisphere). Spanish-language creative is essential and differentiating
- **San Diego measured legal advertising spend is $25-$35M annually** (ATRA 2024) — a fraction of LA, but concentrated among fewer firms, meaning each potential client represents a larger share of market spend
- **The market supports fewer lead gen vendors** due to smaller size, reducing competition for Second Chair

**Key risks:** Smaller market size limits volume ceiling. The DMA only supports 1-2 exclusive territories, capping revenue potential. Some firms may already buy from the same vendors operating in LA.

**Why Tier 3 despite Davis's proximity:** The smaller market size means lower revenue per client. Strategic value is high (easy to service, good margins), but the absolute revenue opportunity is 40-60% of LA or NY. Best deployed as a "fill" market after higher-volume DMAs are established, or as an early proof-of-concept if a warm relationship exists.

**Revenue projection (1 client):** 30-50 leads/month × $400 = $12,000-$20,000 MRR

#### Strategic Rationale

Philadelphia rounds out the Top 10 as an East Coast expansion market accessible from Sasha's New York base.

- **Philadelphia is a 90-minute Amtrak ride from New York Penn Station** — Sasha can service Philly clients with same-day trips, no flights required. This is the closest geographic expansion from the NY base
- **Pennsylvania is a plaintiff-friendly state** with a modified comparative fault system (plaintiff can recover if less than 51% at fault) and no caps on non-economic damages in PI cases
- **Philadelphia County (First Judicial District) is one of the most plaintiff-favorable venues on the East Coast** — historically high verdict averages, sympathetic juries, and progressive damage awards. The Philadelphia Court of Common Pleas has a dedicated complex litigation program for mass tort/PI cases
- **The I-76/I-95/I-476 (Blue Route) corridor** generates significant MVA volume, and the Schuylkill Expressway (I-76) is consistently ranked among the most dangerous highways in Pennsylvania
- **The Delaware Valley PI market** includes Southern New Jersey suburbs (Cherry Hill, Camden County) and Northern Delaware (Wilmington), expanding the effective reach beyond the core Philadelphia DMA
- **Google Ads CPC is moderate** — $150-$320 for PI keywords, giving Second Chair strong margin at $400/lead ($220-$280 per lead)
- **The Philadelphia market has a strong culture of mid-size PI firms** — the 5-20 attorney range that is Second Chair's ideal customer profile. Unlike NY/LA where mega-firms dominate mindshare, Philly's PI landscape is populated by the exact firm size that benefits most from geo-exclusive lead gen

**Key risks:** Pennsylvania's auto insurance system includes a "limited tort" option (cheaper premiums but restricted right to sue for pain/suffering), which complicates lead qualification — must screen for full-tort vs. limited-tort policyholders. No team member is locally based (Sasha is nearest). The market is smaller than Chicago or Houston.

**Mitigation:** The limited-tort screening is a qualification layer that Second Chair can build into the funnel — and it's a pain point that other lead gen vendors don't address, creating differentiation. Sasha's proximity from NY makes this market operationally viable without a local hire.

**Revenue projection (1 client):** 40-60 leads/month × $400 = $16,000-$24,000 MRR

## Key Assumptions and Limitations

1. **CPL estimates are blended projections** based on a multi-channel mix (40% paid search, 35% paid social, 25% programmatic/display). Actual CPL will vary based on campaign maturity, creative performance, and seasonal factors.

2. **Lead volume projections assume mature campaigns** (month 3+ in each market). Months 1-2 will typically run at 40-60% of steady-state volume as campaigns optimize.

3. **Revenue projections assume $400/lead fixed pricing.** Volume discounts, bundle pricing (multi-DMA or statewide), or premium pricing for high-value sub-territories (e.g., Manhattan, Beverly Hills) could shift these numbers.

4. **Competition levels are snapshots** as of February 2026 and will shift as vendors enter/exit markets. Second Chair's geo-exclusive model creates a defensive moat once territories are locked.

5. **Case values are median attorney fee estimates** based on industry data and market-specific adjustments. Actual case values vary widely — a single trucking accident wrongful death case can exceed $1M in attorney fees, while soft tissue cases may net $5,000-$7,000.

6. **Google Ads CPC data** is based on Q4 2025 Keyword Planner estimates for high-intent PI keywords ("car accident lawyer [city]," "personal injury attorney [city]," "truck accident lawyer [city]"). Actual CPCs fluctuate seasonally and competitively.

7. **PI firm counts** are estimated from ABA lawyer demographics, state bar member directories, and legal marketing industry reports. Counts include solo practitioners through mid-size firms actively handling PI cases.
