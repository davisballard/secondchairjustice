# Competitor Geo-Exclusivity Analysis

> **Purpose:** Understand how the four key competitors in the PI lead gen space define, sell, and enforce geo-exclusivity — so we know where the industry standard sits and where Second Chair's model stands out
> **Research date:** February 2026  
> **Sources:** Competitor websites, terms of service, marketing pages (primary research)

---

## The Most Important Finding Upfront

Most companies that sell "exclusive leads" do not sell exclusive territory. These are two completely different things, and the distinction is the most important strategic insight from this research.

- **Exclusive lead** = the same individual lead record isn't sold to two buyers simultaneously
- **Exclusive territory** = no competing firm in your market can buy from this vendor at all

Legal Brand Marketing's own terms of service make this explicit:

> *"Contacts in Kentucky will be delivered to the Participant on an exclusive basis, however, the Participant does not have exclusive territorial rights."*

That means LBM can have Firm A and Firm B — both PI firms in Louisville — both buying "exclusive" leads from LBM at the same time. They'll never get the same lead record. But they are absolutely competing with each other through the same vendor.

Second Chair's model offers something fundamentally different: one firm per market, full stop. The only direct competitor who operates this way is Taqtics.

## Competitor Breakdown

### 1. Taqtics

**Service type:** CTV (Connected TV) advertising + audience platform for PI law firms  
**Website:** taqtics.com  
**Territory unit:** DMA (Designated Market Area)  
**Exclusivity type:** True territory exclusivity — one firm per DMA

**How it works:**
When a firm partners with Taqtics, they secure exclusive access to their DMA's custom audiences. Competing firms cannot access or purchase those audiences within that designated area. Taqtics explicitly states they are "selective" and work with only one firm per market.

**What makes it exclusive:**
- The proprietary audience data built for that DMA is locked to one firm
- Competitors in the same DMA cannot access Taqtics' services at all
- Territory is defined at the DMA level — Taqtics tracks competitive data "across every U.S. DMA" using that as their standard geographic unit

**Key differentiator:**
Taqtics builds custom audience targeting using clinical data (IQVIA, HealthVerity, Komodo Health), life event signals, and intent data. The custom audience is market-specific and can't simply be replicated by a competitor — it's not just lead routing, it's proprietary data infrastructure per market.

**Pricing model:** Not publicly disclosed. Full-service engagement (CTV creative + media placement + attribution).

**What this means for Second Chair:**
Taqtics is our closest competitive analog on territory structure. They use DMA as the unit, we currently use state (Jacoby) or some undefined metro standard. They're CTV-focused; we're paid social + form lead gen. We're not head-to-head on channel, but we are on the territory model. A firm choosing between Taqtics and Second Chair will be evaluating two true territory-exclusive models.

### 2. Legal Brand Marketing (LBM)

**Service type:** Pay-per-lead, multi-practice-area legal lead gen  
**In market:** 20+ years (since 2004)  
**Website:** legalbrandmarketing.com  
**Territory unit:** None — no territory lock exists  
**Exclusivity type:** Lead-level exclusivity only

**How it works:**
LBM operates on a cooperative advertising model. Law firms buy leads in their practice area and state. The leads are exclusive in the sense that the same contact isn't sold twice. But there is no territorial protection — multiple competing firms in the same city can all be buying "exclusive" PI leads from LBM simultaneously.

**From their actual Terms of Service (updated Jan 2026):**
> *"Contacts in Kentucky will be delivered to the Participant on an exclusive basis, however, the Participant does not have exclusive territorial rights."*

The disputable reasons for a lead include "Wrong geographical area" — meaning geography is defined by the lead's location matching the firm's service area, not by a territorial lock.

**Key facts:**
- No long-term contracts — pay-as-you-go, cancel with 5 business days notice
- $97 one-time setup fee
- Lead sources: SEO, PPC, social media, legal directories
- Lead delivery: email, live transfer, SMS, or CRM integration
- 7-day dispute window for bad leads

**What "exclusive" actually means here:**
If you buy PI auto accident leads for California, LBM won't sell the same individual's form submission to another firm. But your competitor down the street who also buys California PI auto leads from LBM is getting different "exclusive" leads — from the same inventory, through the same funnel, targeting the same pool of consumers. You are functionally competing through the same vendor.

**What this means for Second Chair:**
LBM is not a true territory-exclusive competitor. They're a volume lead supplier with lead-level exclusivity. The sales argument is clean: "LBM sells exclusive leads. We sell an exclusive market. Two firms in Los Angeles can both be LBM clients. Only one firm in Los Angeles can be a Second Chair client."

### 3. iLawyer Marketing

**Service type:** Full-service law firm digital marketing agency (SEO, PPC, social, geofencing)  
**Website:** ilawyermarketing.com  
**Territory unit:** None  
**Exclusivity type:** None — this is an agency, not a lead gen vendor with exclusivity

**How it works:**
iLawyer Marketing builds and manages digital marketing campaigns for law firms. Their "geo" offering is geofencing — placing GPS/RFID virtual boundaries around physical locations (hospitals, body shops, courthouses, competitor law firms) and serving retargeted ads to people who enter those locations.

**What geofencing is and isn't:**
- Geofencing is a targeting technique for the firm's own ad campaigns
- It creates virtual perimeters around specific locations and serves ads to mobile devices that enter them
- It is not a lead generation model with exclusivity — multiple competing firms can run geofencing campaigns targeting the same locations
- There is no territorial lock, no one-firm-per-market commitment

**Examples of geofencing targets they use for PI firms:**
- Hospitals and orthopedic clinics (targeting recent injury victims)
- Body shops and collision repair centers
- Chiropractors and physical therapy centers
- Competitor law firms

**What this means for Second Chair:**
iLawyer is not a direct competitor on territory exclusivity. They're a marketing agency that helps firms run their own campaigns. A firm using iLawyer is managing its own marketing independently — they're not buying into an exclusive territory model. Firms might use iLawyer alongside Second Chair; they're not substitutes.

### 4. Ngage (Martindale-Hubbell / Avvo Network)

**Service type:** Live chat software for law firm websites + Direct Leads product  
**Website:** ngagelive.com  
**Network:** Martindale-Hubbell, Avvo, Lawyers.com, Martindale-Nolo (100,000+ consumer requests/month)  
**Territory unit:** None  
**Exclusivity type:** Lead-level exclusivity on Direct Leads product only

**How it works:**
Ngage is primarily a live chat platform. Law firms embed Ngage's 24/7 chat operators on their website. When a visitor starts a chat, Ngage operators collect their contact information and case details, then route to the firm via email, text, or live phone transfer.

Their separate "Direct Leads" product delivers pre-screened, exclusive leads (one lead, one firm) but with no territorial protection.

**The network problem:**
Ngage is part of a multi-property network (Martindale-Hubbell, Avvo, Lawyers.com, Martindale-Nolo). These are legal directory platforms where consumers search for attorneys. When a consumer submits a request on Lawyers.com, they are often contacted by multiple attorneys — exclusivity is limited.

**Key facts:**
- Pay-for-performance model — pay only for relevant leads received
- One-time activation fee
- Practice areas: bankruptcy, criminal defense, SSDI, employment law, family law, personal injury, workers' comp
- Average: 3.76 legitimate live chat conversations to sign first client (per PI firm survey)

**What this means for Second Chair:**
Ngage's core product is website chat infrastructure, not a territory-exclusive lead model. A firm using Ngage is capturing its own inbound website traffic — a completely different funnel than Second Chair's outbound consumer acquisition. Their Direct Leads product has lead-level exclusivity but no territory protection, same as LBM.

## Industry Territory Unit Comparison

The research reveals the industry uses at least four different geographic units for defining exclusivity:

| Unit | Who Uses It | # Available Nationally | Boundary Definition |
|------|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **ZIP Code** | Various smaller vendors | ~42,000 | USPS postal boundaries |
| **County** | Some regional vendors | ~3,143 | County government lines |
| **DMA** | Taqtics | 210 | Nielsen media market boundaries |
| **MSA** | Second Chair (proposed) | 382 | Census Bureau metro definitions |
| **State** | Second Chair (current Jacoby model) | 50 | State government lines |

**Key observations:**
- ZIP and county are too granular for meaningful territory exclusivity at scale — most PI firms serve a full metro, not a ZIP code
- State is too broad — see the California problem (25 metros treated as one territory)
- DMA and MSA are the two serious options at metro scale
- Most competitors who claim "exclusive" leads are operating at the lead level, not any geographic unit

## DMA vs. MSA: The Deciding Factor

The two realistic territory units for Second Chair are DMA and MSA. Here's how they compare:

**DMA (Designated Market Area)**
- 210 DMAs nationally (Nielsen-defined)
- Standard unit for TV, radio, streaming, and CTV advertising
- PI law firms already understand and use DMAs — this is how they buy media
- Fewer, larger markets — LA DMA covers the entire Los Angeles media market including surrounding suburbs
- What Taqtics uses

**MSA (Metropolitan Statistical Area)**
- 382 MSAs nationally (Census Bureau-defined)
- Standard for economic and demographic analysis
- More granular than DMA — captures distinct metro economies
- More units = more clients Second Chair can sell; more revenue ceiling
- Less intuitive to PI firms in advertising context

**Revenue ceiling comparison:**

| Model | Max Clients (Full US) | Avg ARR per Client | Maximum ARR Ceiling |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Per State | 50 | $800K–$1.2M | $40M–$60M |
| Per DMA | 210 | $400K–$800K | $84M–$168M |
| Per MSA | 382 | $300K–$650K | $115M–$248M |

**Recommendation:** DMA is the better standard unit. Here's why:
1. PI firms already understand and budget by DMA — it's how they think about TV and streaming spend
2. It aligns Second Chair's territory language with how clients already talk about their markets
3. Taqtics' use of DMA validates it as a credible industry standard for territory exclusivity
4. 210 DMA slots is still a dramatically better ceiling than 50 state slots
5. DMA boundaries are published and unambiguous — no arguments about whether Pasadena is in the "LA territory"

The MSA model has a higher theoretical ceiling but introduces boundary complexity. If a client asks "what's included in my territory," pointing to the Nielsen DMA map is cleaner than a Census MSA definition.

## What Second Chair Should Say in Sales Conversations

**Against LBM (the most common competitor mention):**

> "Legal Brand Marketing sells exclusive leads — meaning they won't sell the same person's contact info to two firms. But they can, and do, sell leads to two competing PI firms in the same city at the same time. Both firms think they're getting 'exclusive' leads. Neither has the market locked. With Second Chair, you're the only firm in your DMA. Period. Your competitor can't become a Second Chair client in your market."

**Against Taqtics:**

> "Taqtics also does one firm per market — respect to them for that. The difference is channel: they're CTV-focused, which means brand awareness and top-of-funnel. We generate bottom-of-funnel leads — people who already had an accident and are actively seeking an attorney right now. Different tools, both with real exclusivity. Some firms use both."

**Against iLawyer:**

> "iLawyer runs your own marketing campaigns for you. That's a legitimate service. We're a different model entirely — we generate the leads, qualify them, and deliver them to you exclusively. You don't manage campaigns; you just answer the phone."
