## Behavioral Science Validation

Your approach is backed by 15+ studies in the Behavioral Library:

### Identity Salience Effect
*Forehand, DeshpandÃ© & Reed, 2002*

### Cialdini's Localized Norms
*Hotel Towel Experiment, 2008*
> "Localized norms ('people in this room') outperformed generic. Hyper-relevant social proof wins."

**Application:** "People in Bend who drive on Highway 97" > "People in Oregon" > "People who drive"

The more hyper-local, the more stopping power.

### Self-Verification Theory
*Swann, 1983-1996*
> "People seek to confirm existing self-views. Messages consistent with identity are more persuasive."

**Application:** When people see themselves accurately depictedâ€”including the clichÃ©sâ€”they lean in. It's recognition, not mockery.

---

### Frijda's Law of Apparent Reality
*1988*
> "The intensity of emotion depends on how real or likely an event seems. Use concrete imagery and relatable scenarios."

**Application:** 
- Generic "car accident victim" = abstract
- **45-year-old Bend local in a North Face vest who just hit black ice on Galveston Avenue** = viscerally real

---

### Von Restorff Effect (Distinctiveness)
*Capaldi & Neath, 2000*
> "Distinctive items are remembered better than uniform ones. Contrast in context boosts memory."

**Application:** A generic "person on phone" = invisible. A **very specific person who looks like they shop at REI, drives a Tacoma, and has never missed a Sunday farmers market** = distinctive, memorable.

## Scroll-Stopping Power: How to "Ramp It Up"

### The Compliant Ways to Maximize Impact

#### 1. FACE + DIRECT EYE CONTACT
From approved imagery: *"Face with emotionâ€”humans are wired to look at faces"*

**The Ramp-Up:** Instead of looking away, have them **look directly at camera with a knowing expression**. Not distressâ€”**recognition**.

---

#### 2. HYPER-SPECIFIC ENVIRONMENT CUES
From Cognitive Ease research: *"Contextual cues improve recall"*

**The Ramp-Up:** 
- âŒ Generic "mountain background"
- âœ… **That specific intersection everyone knows**
- âœ… **The exact parking lot at the local landmark**
- âœ… **Standing in front of the local brewery/coffee shop**

---

#### 3. THE "PATTERN INTERRUPT" COMPOSITION
From Approved_Imagery.md: *"Unusual composition creates pattern interrupt"*

**Options:**
- Extreme close-up on face (unusual for legal ads)
- Dutch angle (tilted frame)
- Person walking toward camera
- Unexpected crop (cut off top of head)
- "Caught mid-action" not posed

---

#### 4. TEXT OVERLAY WITH IDENTITY CALL-OUT
From Cognitive Ease: *"Text overlay creates curiosity gap"*

**Templates:**
- "Every [City] driver has been here..."
- "If you know, you know..."
- "POV: You drive [Local Road] every weekend"
- "[City] [archetype]: This is for you..."

---

#### 5. HIGH CONTRAST COLOR POPS
From Color Weapons: *"Contrast matters more than the specific color"*

**The Ramp-Up:**
- One **bright element** (orange vest, red jacket, yellow hat) against neutral background
- That element draws the eye FIRST
- Use `#FF4500` or `#E63946` for text overlays

## Pre-Production Checklist

Before producing any "Starter Pack" image:

### The Person
- [ ] Age range is specific (not generic)
- [ ] Clothing matches regional identity
- [ ] Accessories match lifestyle archetype
- [ ] Expression is confident/neutral/knowing (not distressed)
- [ ] No visible injuries, casts, medical equipment

### The Environment
- [ ] Location is recognizable to locals
- [ ] No accident scene visible
- [ ] Positive or neutral context
- [ ] Vehicle visible but undamaged

### The Composition
- [ ] At least ONE scroll-stopping element (direct eye contact, unusual crop, color pop)
- [ ] Documentary/candid style (not stock photo)
- [ ] High contrast for feed visibility

### The Text
- [ ] Third-person framing
- [ ] No "you" + injury/condition
- [ ] Identity activation (location, archetype, lifestyle)
- [ ] Curiosity gap or insider framing
