## How AI Models Select Sources

### What Gets Cited

| Factor | Weight | How to Optimize |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Authoritative Source** | High | Clear entity, consistent NAP, topic expertise |
| **Clear, Quotable Answers** | High | Direct answers in first paragraph, no fluff |
| **Structured Content** | High | Headers as questions, numbered lists, tables |
| **Recency** | Medium | Fresh content, updated dates |
| **Factual Accuracy** | High | Cite statutes, official sources |
| **E-E-A-T Signals** | High | Author credentials, about page, trust signals |

### What Gets Ignored

- Salesy, promotional content
- Vague or generic answers
- Content hidden behind paywalls
- Thin content without substance
- Content that reads like ads

## Content Strategy for AI Citation

### Priority Content Types

#### Tier 1: Definitive Guides (Highest Citation Potential)

These become the "source of truth" AI models cite:

| Topic | Target Query | Content Structure |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| Post-Accident Checklist | "what to do after car accident [State]" | Numbered steps, timeline, dos/don'ts |
| Statute of Limitations | "[State] car accident lawsuit deadline" | Clear dates, exceptions, table format |
| Insurance Claim Process | "how to file car accident insurance claim" | Step-by-step, common mistakes |
| Compensation Types | "what can I sue for after car accident" | Categorized list with explanations |
| Fault Determination | "who is at fault in rear-end collision" | State-specific rules, exceptions |

**Content Template:**
```
[Direct answer to question in first 2 sentences]

## Quick Answer
[Bulleted summary — 3-5 points AI can extract]

## Detailed Breakdown
[Comprehensive explanation with headers for each sub-topic]

## [State]-Specific Rules
[Localized information — major differentiator]

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
[Loss aversion framing — behavioral science integration]

## Next Steps
[CTA to quiz funnel — soft, helpful framing]
```

#### Tier 2: FAQ Content (High Volume, Medium Effort)

Structured Q&A that AI can directly extract:

| Question Pattern | Examples |
|------------------|----------|
| "How long..." | How long do I have to file a claim in Nevada? |
| "Can I sue if..." | Can I sue if the accident was partially my fault? |
| "Do I need..." | Do I need a lawyer for a minor car accident? |
| "What happens if..." | What happens if the other driver has no insurance? |
| "How much..." | How much is my car accident case worth? |

**Structure each FAQ as:**
1. Question as H2 header
2. Direct answer in first sentence
3. 2-3 paragraph explanation
4. State-specific callout if applicable
5. Related questions (internal linking)

#### Tier 3: Comparison & Decision Content

Content that helps users make decisions (high commercial intent):

- "Lawyer vs. handling claim yourself"
- "When to accept insurance settlement"
- "Signs you need a car accident lawyer"
- "How lawyers get paid in accident cases"

## Technical Optimization for AI

### Schema Markup (Structured Data)

Implement on all Claim Justice Now content:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "What should I do immediately after a car accident in Nevada?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "After a Nevada car accident: 1) Check for injuries and call 911, 2) Exchange information with other drivers, 3) Document the scene with photos, 4) Report to police within 10 days if over $750 damage, 5) Notify your insurance, 6) Consult with a personal injury attorney within the 2-year statute of limitations."
    }
  }]
}
```

### Key Schema Types to Implement

| Content Type | Schema | Purpose |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| FAQ pages | FAQPage | Direct answer extraction |
| How-to guides | HowTo | Step extraction |
| State guides | Article + LocalBusiness | Geographic relevance |
| About/Trust pages | Organization | Entity clarity |

### Entity Optimization

Make Claim Justice Now a clear, citable entity:

1. **Consistent NAP** — Name, Address, Phone consistent everywhere
2. **Wikipedia-style About page** — Factual, third-person description
3. **Author pages** — If publishing content, create author bios
4. **Topic clustering** — Become THE source for specific topics
5. **Cross-references** — Link from high-authority sites

## Forum & UGC Strategy

### Why Forums Matter for AI

AI models heavily weight:
- **Reddit** — r/legaladvice, r/insurance, r/personalfinance, state subreddits
- **Quora** — Legal advice questions
- **Avvo** — Legal Q&A platform
- **Justia** — Legal community forums

### Approach (Ethical, Not Gaming)

1. **Be genuinely helpful** — Answer questions with real value
2. **Disclose appropriately** — Don't pretend to be something you're not
3. **Link naturally** — Only when content genuinely helps
4. **Build reputation** — Consistent, helpful presence over time

### What NOT to Do
- Spam links
- Create fake accounts
- Give specific legal advice (you're not a law firm)
- Astroturf reviews or recommendations
