## John Hegarty's Three-Test Filter

### Test 1: CLARITY — "Do I understand what you're selling immediately?"

**Verdict:** ✅✅✅ PASS (9/10)

**What Works:**
- "We don't just sell leads" — immediate differentiation
- "One firm per market" — crystal clear positioning
- "Geo-exclusive demand infrastructure" — precise description

**John's Assessment:** "I know exactly what you do within 3 seconds. That's rare. Most B2B sites bury the lead. You don't. Excellent."

### Test 2: ZAG — "Is this different from everyone else?"

**Verdict:** ⚠️ CONDITIONAL PASS (7/10)

**Verbal Differentiation:** ✅ Strong
- Geo-exclusive model (vs. shared/auction leads)
- Selective positioning ("We don't work with every firm")
- Honesty signaling ("Bad weeks reported early")

**Visual Differentiation:** ⚠️ Good but not memorable
- Clean vs. competitors' busy layouts (differentiated)
- Minimal vs. stock lawyer photos (differentiated)
- But: Still looks like a premium SaaS site, not a legal partner

**John's Assessment:** "You've zagged in positioning, but the visual execution is safe. The typography is confident. The colors are restrained. But where's the visual idea that makes me remember you tomorrow? The territory map concept could be that idea—if it's bold enough."

**The Missing Zag:**
- **Verbal zag exists:** Geo-exclusive, selective, transparent
- **Visual zag weak:** Clean but generic premium design
- **Conceptual zag needed:** Territory ownership should be visualized dramatically

### Test 3: COURAGE — "Did you take a risk, or play it safe?"

**Verdict:** ⚠️ CONDITIONAL PASS (7/10)

**Courage Moments:**
✅ "We don't work with every firm" — risky selectivity  
✅ "Bad weeks reported early" — vulnerable honesty  
✅ Minimal design in busy category — restraint takes guts  

**Safe Moments:**
⚠️ Green CTA color — polite, not commanding  
⚠️ No founder story — hiding behind corporate brand  
⚠️ No competitive comparisons — avoiding confrontation  
⚠️ No bold visual metaphor — playing design safe  

**John's Assessment:** "You're courageous in copy—saying 'we don't work with everyone' is ballsy. But the design is playing it safe. The green CTAs whisper when they should shout. The territory map idea is there, but I bet it's going to be tasteful when it should be dramatic. Be as bold visually as you are verbally."

## Visual/Verbal Marriage Assessment

### Where They Work Together:

**Hero Section:**
- Verbal: "We don't just sell leads"
- Visual: Bold, assertive typography
- **Marriage:** 7/10 (typography matches tone, but no visual metaphor)

**Geo-Exclusive Section:**
- Verbal: "One firm per market. Once your market is locked, it's locked."
- Visual: Clean section with body copy
- **Marriage:** 5/10 ❌ ("Locked market" begs for visual proof—map missing)

**Four-Phase System:**
- Verbal: "Four phases. One system."
- Visual: Numbered sections (01, 02, 03, 04)
- **Marriage:** 8/10 (numbers create rhythm, supports "system" concept)

**Overall Marriage Quality:** 6.5/10
- Typography confidence matches verbal confidence
- But concepts like "territory" and "locked markets" have no visual expression
- The "infrastructure" idea is never visualized

**John's Fix:** "Add the territory map. Make it big, bold, and impossible to miss. Show the locked territories in green. Show available territories fading out. Make people **feel** the scarcity. That's the visual idea this site is missing."

**Current Contenders:**
1. "We don't just sell leads." (strong, but generic)
2. "One firm per market." (clearer, more distinctive)
