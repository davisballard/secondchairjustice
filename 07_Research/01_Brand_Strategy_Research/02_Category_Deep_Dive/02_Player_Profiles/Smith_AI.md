## What They Are

Smith.ai does not generate leads. They convert them. A 24/7 hybrid AI-plus-human virtual receptionist service that answers calls, screens callers, conducts intake, and schedules appointments on behalf of law firms — ensuring that no inbound lead goes unanswered regardless of the time of day.

Their relevance to the MVA lead gen category: they sit at the critical Stage 4 conversion layer. The single largest variable in lead conversion (across all vendors, all lead types) is how fast the attorney's firm responds to inbound contacts. Smith.ai addresses this structural failure in the industry.

**The market opportunity they're addressing:**
- 61% of PI inquiries come by phone
- The industry median response time is 13 minutes (Session 01 research)
- 26% of law firm inquiries never receive a returned call
- Firms responding within 60 seconds convert at 391% higher rates than firms waiting 5+ minutes
- Missing a single PI caller costs a firm $18,000–$20,000 in lost case value

## What They Sell

**Virtual Receptionist Plans (Law Firm Focus):**
24/7 live answering with North America-based agents. AI handles initial screening; human agents take over for complex calls. Services include:
- Call answering and intake
- Lead screening and qualification (up to 10 custom intake questions)
- Appointment scheduling
- CRM sync and lead delivery
- Call recordings and transcripts
- After-hours and weekend coverage
- Bilingual (Spanish) support available

**Smith.ai Outreach:**
An outbound follow-up tool — Smith.ai agents call back web form leads on behalf of the firm within minutes of submission. This directly addresses the 13-minute median response problem and the contact rate collapse in shared lead markets.

**CRM/Software Integrations:** Filevine, Calendly, Clio, and others.

## Business Model Analysis

**How they make money:** SaaS subscriptions at tiered price points. AI reduces COGS — the AI handles initial call screening before human agents intervene, which means human labor cost is reserved for high-value interactions.

**The hybrid AI/human bet:** Pure AI phone agents (like Vocodia) are emerging but create discomfort among claimants who want to feel like they're being heard. Pure human intake centers (like Alert Communications) are reliable but expensive. Smith.ai's hybrid model threads the needle — AI handles routing and screening, humans handle empathy and complexity.

**The category integration play:** By building native integrations with Filevine, Clio, and other legal CRMs, Smith.ai becomes sticky. Replacing them requires re-integrating the firm's entire intake workflow. This integration moat is substantial.

**Their non-legal constraint:** Smith.ai serves multiple industries (home services, medical, legal, and others). This means their intake specialists are generalists, not legal specialists. Alert Communications (legal-only since 1965) and Legal Conversion Center (PI-specific) may have intake specialists with deeper legal qualification training.

---

## Their Model's Weaknesses

1. **Not legal-exclusive:** Intake agents serve industries from home services to legal. Legal qualification requires nuanced understanding of SOL, fault determination, and injury classification that general agents may lack.

2. **Overage cost scaling:** At the Pro plan ($825/month, 300 calls), a high-volume PI firm receiving 400+ calls/month faces significant overage at $3.75/call above 300 = an additional $375 minimum monthly. Budget predictability is a limitation.

3. **Doesn't generate leads:** Smith.ai's value is entirely dependent on having lead flow coming in. Firms with a volume problem (not enough leads) don't solve that problem with Smith.ai — they need a lead vendor first, then Smith.ai to convert what arrives.

4. **AI quality floor:** On complex intakes — injured claimants who are distressed, confused, or speaking limited English — AI screening may create a negative first impression that damages conversion before a human agent gets involved.
