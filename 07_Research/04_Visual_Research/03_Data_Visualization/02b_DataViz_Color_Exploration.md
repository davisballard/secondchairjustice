# Second Chair — DataViz Color Palette Exploration

**Session goal:** Find a data visualization palette that harmonizes with the locked core brand without reading as generic or ugly. Five directions below — each with 6 categorical series colors and status mapping. Use Color Highlight extension to see swatches inline.

## DIRECTION A — DEEP JEWEL

**Positioning:** Rich gemstone tones. Every color has the weight of a physical material — sapphire, emerald, claret, topaz, amethyst, bronze. Elevated, clearly distinguishable, zero generic SaaS energy.

**Feel:** A Victorian naturalist's specimen cabinet. Deep, saturated, serious.

| Series | Name | Hex | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sapphire | `#1B4F8A` | Deep royal blue. Anchor series. |
| 2 | Emerald | `#1A6B45` | Forest emerald. Rich, botanical. |
| 3 | Claret | `#7A1E3A` | Deep red-purple. Distinct from oxblood. |
| 4 | Topaz | `#B5740A` | Warm amber-gold. Distinct from brand gold. |
| 5 | Amethyst | `#5C3272` | Deep purple. Rare, distinguished. |
| 6 | Bronze | `#7A5C2E` | Warm brown-bronze. Earthy anchor. |

### Status Mapping

| Status | Color | Hex | Why |
|---|---|---|---|

| Caution | Topaz | `#B5740A` | Amber register, not neon yellow |

## SESSION NOTES

- Direction A (Deep Jewel) and Direction C (Ivy) are most directly adjacent to the brand world — they share the same cultural reference points as the core palette
- Direction B (British Institutional) is the most restrained and would produce the most Economist-like charts
- Direction D (Print Ink) would feel most authentic to the 1980s publication reference — these are literally the colors offset printing produced
- Direction E (Architectural Earth) is the most unusual — could be highly distinctive but risks being too muted for clear categorical differentiation

**Decision:** Pick a direction (or a hybrid of two). Can mix individual series colors across directions once the anchor is chosen.
