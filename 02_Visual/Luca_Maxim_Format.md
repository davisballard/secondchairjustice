### Why This Format Works

Every character â€” villain insurance executive, hero accident victim â€” is built with **starter pack precision**. Not "a business man" but THE insurance villain where every single detail screams recognition. Not "a working mom" but THE exact working-class family archetype for your target market.

### Starter Pack Precision (Observant, Not Derogatory)

This isn't mockery. It's **paying homage by nailing every detail.** Like a great portrait artist who captures essence, we capture cultural archetypes with such precision that viewers feel seen.

## âš ï¸ MOTION & ENERGY: EVERYTHING IS DRAMATIC

### Nothing Is Subtle. Nothing Is Slow.

Luca's videos are ALIVE. Every action is performed with maximum dramatic energy. The emotion dial is set to 10.

| Wrong (Generic AI) | Right (Luca Maxim) |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| "Slowly reaches for stamp" | "SLAMS stamp down hard and fast, repeatedly, with force" |
| "Subtle handheld movement" | "Chaotic motion, people laughing, pointing, animated" |
| "Gently picks up document" | "Snatches document, waves it, points aggressively" |
| "Calm office scene" | "Cackling lawyers, table-slapping, high energy chaos" |
| "Subject stands still" | "Subject is the eye of the storm OR the source of dramatic action" |

### The Center Subject Rule

**Option A: The Still Eye of the Storm**
- Everyone AROUND them is in chaotic motion â€” laughing, pointing, slapping table
- The center subject remains composed, watching with quiet satisfaction  
- The CONTRAST between stillness and chaos = power

**Option B: The Source of Drama**
- The subject IS the action â€” slamming, gesturing, performing
- Motion is big, theatrical, exaggerated
- Nothing subtle â€” every movement is a PERFORMANCE

### Motion Energy Principles

**Group Shots â€” Staggered Natural Movement:**
- People should NOT move in synchronized unison â€” that looks robotic
- Movements should RIPPLE through the group at staggered moments
- Use timing cues: "FIRST... A BEAT LATER... THEN... MEANWHILE..."
- Each person moves at their OWN moment, their OWN rhythm
- The energy flows organically, not mechanically

**The Still Center Rule:**
- When one subject is the focus, they can remain STILL while chaos surrounds them
- The contrast between stillness and surrounding motion = POWER
- The still subject is the eye of the storm, the king watching his machine

**Solo Action â€” Maximum Force:**
- Actions are FAST, HARD, DECISIVE â€” never gentle or slow
- Repeated actions escalate in intensity (slam, SLAM, SLAM HARDER)
- Physical impact should be visible (papers shift, things ripple)
- The violence/force of action tells the emotional story

**Camera Movement â€” Dynamic, Not Passive:**
- Cameras SWEEP, ARC, PUSH, DIVE â€” never just "slowly pan"
- Match camera energy to scene energy
- The camera has PURPOSE and DIRECTION, not just observation
- Faster camera = more dramatic energy

### The Emotion Dial = 10/10

| Action | Low Energy (Wrong) | High Energy (Luca Maxim) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| **Laughing** | Slight smile | Mouth WIDE open, head thrown back, belly laugh |
| **Pointing** | Gesturing vaguely | Jabbing finger aggressively |
| **Denying Claims** | Stamping once | SLAMMING stamp repeatedly, rapid fire until paper tears |
| **Scheming** | Looking at papers | Huddle, whisper, break into cackling |
| **Contempt** | Slight smirk | Sneer, eye roll, dismissive wave |
| **Support** | Standing nearby | Hand on shoulder with FIRM reassuring grip |
| **Celebration** | Arms raised | Arms THRUST skyward, cheering, embracing |

### Timing & Pacing

**Action Placement:**
- If an action should trigger a response (button tap, etc.), place it at the END of the clip
- The video ends on the action â€” the response is implied by the cut
- Build anticipation in the first 80% of the clip, action in the final 20%

**Static Elements in Animation:**
- When screens, text, or documents must stay readable, mark them as FROZEN/STATIC
- Only the real-world elements (hands, camera, environment) should move
- Explicitly state "do NOT animate" for elements that must stay unchanged

**If your Kling prompt doesn't have dynamic verbs and energy descriptors â€” it's too subtle.**

## SHOT TYPE STRATEGIES

### Army/Group Shots
When showing groups of people (villain teams, hero teams, crowds):
- The group should SURROUND and FRAME the central subject/family
- Each person in the group needs distinct action/expression â€” not clones
- Create a protective formation (heroes) or threatening formation (villains)
- The MORE people you add, the more power is conveyed
- Groups should have visible diversity (age, ethnicity, gender, role)
- Stagger their movements so they don't look like synchronized robots

### Power Contrast Shots
Every hero shot should CONTRAST with the villain shots:

| Villain Element | Hero Contrast |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Cold fluorescent lighting | Warm golden hour light |
| Sterile glass/steel | Warm wood/leather |
| Looking DOWN at victims | Standing WITH/around family |
| Identical grey suits | Diverse, individual but unified |
| Empty soulless office | Awards, family photos, warmth |
| Gold flashy accessories | Understated, quality |
| Denial stamps everywhere | Victory headlines, "WON" files |
| Cackling contempt | Fierce protective determination |

### Scale Shots (Stadiums, Crowds, Masses)
When showing massive scale (100,000 people, etc.):
- Use real landmarks from the target market without specific branding
- Golden hour light makes crowds feel warm and triumphant
- Include the key number/message ON THE FIELD or center of the space
- The crowd IS the proof â€” every seat filled
- Diversity reflects demographics of the target market
- Balance dramatic energy with photorealistic grounding â€” avoid looking "too AI"
- Let the SCALE speak rather than adding excessive confetti/fireworks

### Absurdist Object Shots
For shots that reveal truth through a single detail:
- The object should be hyperrealistic, almost documentary style
- It tells the WHOLE story in one visual (worn stamp = millions of denials)
- Place it in mundane context that makes the implication more horrifying
- The camera "discovers" it like a documentary finding evidence
- The villain's hand should be reaching for or actively using the object

### CTA/Action Shots
For the final call-to-action:
- Over-the-shoulder framing puts viewer in the subject's position
- The phone/screen takes up majority of frame for visibility
- Keep screen content STATIC and readable â€” no animation on text
- The decisive action (tap, click) happens at the END of the clip
- Build anticipation through the shot, payoff in final moment
- Warm lighting from hero scenes carries through to CTA

## ENVIRONMENT STRATEGIES

### Villain Environments
- Cold, harsh, institutional lighting (fluorescent, blue tint)
- Glass, steel, grey tones â€” no warmth anywhere
- Oversized furniture showing power and compensation
- No personal touches â€” sterile, corporate, soulless
- Floor-to-ceiling windows showing they're ABOVE everyone
- Visible evidence of denial (stamps, files, lowball numbers crossed out)
- Props that visualize cruelty (miniature victims, worn deny stamps)

### Hero Environments
- Warm, golden, natural lighting (golden hour)
- Wood paneling, leather furniture, burgundy/navy tones
- Professional but WELCOMING â€” success with humanity
- Success markers that prove RESULTS (verdict headlines, recovered amounts)
- Personal touches (family photos, client victory photos, fresh flowers)
- Market-specific markers (state flag, local landscape through window, regional pride)
- Awards showing COMPETENCE not just wealth

### Camera Movement Philosophy
- Cameras SWEEP, ARC, PUSH, DIVE â€” never just "slowly pan"
- Match camera energy to scene energy
- The camera has PURPOSE and DIRECTION, not passive observation
- Dynamic movement through space, not static observation
- Faster camera = more dramatic energy
