# Banned Imagery (RED Zone)

> **Source:** Compliance + Behavioral Science + Industry Research (2025-2026)
> 
> These visuals will get ads rejected, accounts restricted, OR kill performance even if approved.

---

## The Neuroscience of Why These Fail

Understanding Kahneman's System 1 (fast, emotional) vs System 2 (slow, logical) processing is critical:

- **System 1 triggers work:** Faces, checks, notifications process in milliseconds
- **Trauma imagery backfires:** Danger signals trigger freeze response, not action
- **The key insight:** You want System 1 arousal that leads to click, not paralysis

> **The core problem with banned imagery:** It activates the reptilian "danger" response but provides no clear escape path. Fear without efficacy = paralysis, not conversion.

---

## Why This Matters (Behavioral Science)

Beyond platform compliance, banned imagery fails for psychological reasons:

| Problem | Behavioral Principle | Effect |
|---------|---------------------|--------|
| Trauma/distress imagery | **Fear without Efficacy** (Janis & Feshbach) | Fear paralyzes without clear solution |
| Graphic content | **Negativity Bias Overload** (Baumeister) | Too much negative = avoidance |
| Exploitation feeling | **Reactance** (Brehm) | Perceived manipulation = rejection |
| Victimhood focus | **Loss of Agency** | People want empowerment, not pity |

**The insight:** Show the story from the *recovery chapter*, not the trauma chapter.

## Instant Rejection (Auto-Reject)

### Blood & Visible Injuries
- Blood (any amount, any context)
- Bruises visible on skin
- Open wounds, cuts, scrapes
- Visible broken bones or deformities
- Bandages with blood showing through

**Why banned:** Meta Chapter 13 (Sensational Content) â€” triggers "exploitation" flags.

---

### Trauma & Distress
- Hospital beds with patient in distress
- Crash footage (real or simulated) with visible damage to people
- Crying/distressed faces looking directly at camera
- Before/after injury comparisons
- Person in visible pain or anguish
- Victim at accident scene

**Why banned:** Personal Attributes + Sensational Content = algorithm death.

**Behavioral insight:** Showing distress without solution activates fear paralysis, not action.

> **Saul says:** "Meta's algorithm sees this and thinks 'exploitation.' And when the algorithm thinks exploitation, it doesn't send a warning â€” it sends an executioner."

## Proceed with Caution (YELLOW Zone)

These may get flagged. Use carefully and monitor CPMs.

### Emergency Imagery
- Emergency vehicle lights (can trigger "sensational")
- Ambulance scenes
- Police at accident scene

**Risk:** May trigger sensational content filter.
**Mitigation:** Lights only, no people, use as distant background.

---

### Medical Equipment
- Wheelchairs (can imply disability = personal attribute)
- Hospital equipment prominently featured
- Medical devices in distressing context

**Risk:** Can trigger healthcare filter even for legal ads.
**Safe alternatives:** Cast with signatures (recovery context), crutches leaning unused.

---

### Money/Cash Imagery
- Stacks of cash
- Money raining down
- "Jackpot" style imagery

**Risk:** Appears scammy, can trigger financial services flags.
**Alternative:** Simple number comparisons or calculator displays.

**What it is:** Ads styled like smartphone notifications showing "Direct Deposit Received: $25,000"

- Must be clearly stylized (not exact iOS/Android UI)

### ðŸŸ¡ "Breaking News" / News Chyron Format

**What it is:** Ads styled like news broadcasts with tickers, "BREAKING" banners, red/yellow alert colors

**Why it's tempting:** Borrows authority of news media, bypasses ad skepticism

**Why it's risky:**
- Can trigger "fake news" / misinformation policies
- Platform crackdowns on news mimicry
- Must contain factual, verifiable information only

**If using:**
- Only for mass torts with actual news coverage
- Never fabricate "news" that doesn't exist
- Keep styling clearly differentiated from actual news
- Include clear advertising disclosures

---

### ðŸŸ¡ AI-Generated "Realistic" Imagery

**The "Uncanny Valley" paradox:** Slight AI artifacts can actually HELP performance by contributing to "amateur" aesthetic. BUT:

**Risks:**
- Obvious AI hands/text = credibility killer
- "Too perfect" faces = stock photo association = distrust
- Platform policies evolving on AI disclosure

**Safe zone for AI:**
- Scenes, backgrounds, documents, objects
- Situations where imperfection is expected (lo-fi)

**Danger zone for AI:**
- Photorealistic faces making claims
- Fake "testimonial" style imagery
- Anything implying real person/real result

### ðŸ”´ AI Lip-Sync / Deepfake Video (BANNED)

> **Critical for Jacoby & Meyers compliance:** Meta actively detects AI lip-sync and flags accounts for "Unacceptable Business Practices." This is an account-level ban risk, not just ad rejection.

**What is BANNED:**
- AI-generated video of a person appearing to speak testimonial content
- Lip-sync AI making someone appear to say "I got $50k thanks to..."
- Any video where AI makes it appear a real person is giving a testimonial
- Using image-to-video models to animate faces with speech

**Why this is catastrophic:**
- Meta's detection of synthetic speech is sophisticated and improving
- "Unacceptable Business Practices" = potential permanent account ban
- Jacoby & Meyers as a regulated law firm has zero tolerance for this

**The rule:** Use AI for the VISUALS (scenes, cars, documents, environments), not the EVIDENCE (testimonials, client statements, results claims).

**Safe alternatives:**
- Text overlays on AI-generated visuals
- Voiceover narration over AI scenes (voice doesn't claim to BE the person in video)
- Real spokesperson with AI-generated B-roll backgrounds
- AI generates the setting, human provides the message
