## The "Polish Penalty": Why Ugly Ads Win

### The Counter-Intuitive Truth

Industry research consistently shows that high-production-value ads (studio lighting, 4K resolution, professional actors) generate **higher CPMs and lower CTRs** than "ugly" lo-fi ads.

**Data Point:** Case studies show UGC-style iPhone video outperforming $2,000 studio productions by 3:1 in CTR and CPA.

### Why This Happens

| Polish Level | Brain Response | Result |
|--------------|----------------|--------|
| High production | "Someone is trying to SELL me" | Defenses UP, scroll past |
| Lo-fi/authentic | "Someone is trying to TELL me" | Defenses DOWN, engagement |

This is "Banner Blindness 2.0" â€” users have learned to tune out anything that looks like high-end media because it disrupts their native feed experience.

### Visual Characteristics of Winning "Ugly" Ads

| Element | Do This | Not This |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Lighting** | Natural, uneven, shadows, dashboard glow | Three-point studio lighting |
| **Composition** | "Bad" framing, too close, slightly off-center | Perfect rule-of-thirds |
| **Typography** | Platform-native fonts (TikTok/IG story text) | Burnt-in professional subtitles |
| **Quality** | Smartphone-shot texture | 4K cinematic |
| **Setting** | Kitchen table, car interior, office desk | Studio backdrop |

### The "Lo-Fi Trust Signal" in Legal

In the legal vertical specifically, "slick" = "predatory." The "ambulance chaser" stereotype is associated with loud suits and aggressive commercials.

**The reframe:** Lo-fi suggests "budget goes into the settlement, not the advertising."

## The Neuroscience of Face Imagery

### Why Faces Matter (Pareidolia)

Humans are evolutionarily hardwired to prioritize faces. In lead gen, the **type** of face and **direction of gaze** are critical variables.

### Authenticity Over Aesthetics

| Face Type | Signal | Performance |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Overly symmetrical, airbrushed | "Stock photo" (deception) | LOW trust, LOW CTR |
| Skin texture, asymmetry, genuine emotion | "Peer" (truth) | HIGH trust, HIGH CTR |
| "Model" face | "This is an ad" | Defenses activated |
| "Lived-in" face (tired, stressed, relieved) | "Someone like me" | Empathy activated |

**The insight:** In PI ads, using faces that look tired, stressed, or relieved **mirrors the emotional state of the accident victim** â€” creating instant recognition.

### Gaze Cueing (Eye-Tracking Research)

| Gaze Direction | User Behavior | Use For |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| Direct at camera | Attention stays on face | Emotional connection, trust-building |
| Looking at text/button | Gaze follows to that element | Directing to CTA |
| "Averted shock" (looking at something off-screen) | Creates curiosity gap | Hook moments |

**High-performing pattern:** Person looking in shock at something (implied settlement amount, document, phone) â€” viewer's eye follows to the conversion element.

### AI Face Generation Warning

âš ï¸ **Critical:** AI-generated photorealistic faces are problematic:

- "Too perfect" = stock photo association = distrust
- Uncanny valley effects kill credibility
- Platform policies evolving on AI face disclosure
- Implied testimonial without real person = compliance risk

**Safe alternative:** Use AI for scenes/backgrounds, use stylized or abstract human representation, or wait for real creator partnerships.

# AI Prompting Guide: "Amateur" Realism

> **The paradox:** Slight AI artifacts can actually HELP performance by contributing to the "amateur" aesthetic. As long as the emotional focal point is clear, imperfections validate "realness."

## Prompt Engineering for Lo-Fi Authenticity

### Keywords That Create "Ugly" Aesthetic
Use these to avoid the AI "polish penalty":
- `low resolution`, `motion blur`, `bad lighting`
- `camera flash`, `grainy`, `iPhone photo`
- `candid moment`, `not posing`, `authentic`
- `messy background`, `natural lighting`
- `documentary style`, `raw`, `unedited`

### Negative Prompts (What to Avoid)
Add these to your negative prompts:
- `studio lighting`, `perfect composition`
- `professional lighting`, `4k`, `cinematic`
- `rendered`, `3d`, `clean`, `polished`
- `stock photo`, `watermark`
- `advertising style`, `commercial`
