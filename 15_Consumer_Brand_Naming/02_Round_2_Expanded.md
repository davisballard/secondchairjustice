## DAVIS'S BRIEF AMENDMENT

Three questions from Davis after reviewing the Round 1 shortlist:

**"Does it have to be 'claim'?"**

Good instinct, and the research backs it. Maria Delgado does not think of herself as someone with a "claim." She thinks of herself as someone dealing with the accident, the insurance situation, this whole thing. "Claim" is what the adjuster says. It's also a crowded naming space — ClaimPilot, ClaimYourAccident, ClaimAlgo are all operating in the "[Claim][X]" structure. And "Claim" + "Now" starts to echo existing competitors. Round 2 opens the noun.

**"Does it have to be 'Now'?"**

"Now" works — but Davis asked whether other urgency structures exist that don't feel manipulative. "Now" can tip into "ACT NOW" billboard territory. The goal is the right-moment feeling: *before you decide, do this first.* The word "First" is the strongest alternative: it carries temporal priority without pressure. Also explored: names where urgency is built into the meaning, not appended as a word.

**"More plays on the honest thing."**

Davis likes the register, not necessarily the word "honest." "Honest" has been captured by health and organic brands — Honest Company, Honest Tea. Alternative honesty words to generate across: **Real, Frank, True, Level, Plain, Straight.** Each carries the register without the contamination.

## SUB-DIRECTION A: "Case" Replaces "Claim" — Honest Register + Now or First

**Jon Steel frames the territory:**

*"'Case' is better than 'claim' for this audience. It's lay language — 'you have a case,' 'what's my case worth,' 'my friend took her case to a lawyer.' Nobody says 'I filed a claim' in regular conversation. They say 'I dealt with the insurance' or 'I took my case somewhere.' 'Case' also has a secondary meaning — a case to be made, a case for fairness — that 'claim' doesn't have. And it's not yet owned in the B2C legal lead space the way 'Claim' is.*

*The honest-register adjectives — Real, Frank, True, Level, Plain, Straight — replace 'Honest' without the health brand problem. Ed, generate across all of them. And test both 'Now' and 'First' as the urgency word for each."*

**Ed McCabe — Raw Generation:**

|---|---|---|---|

| 3 | **Frank Case** | Frank Case Now / Frank Case First | "Frank" = no pretense, no games. Also a first name — personable, direct. |

| 6 | **Straight Case** | Straight Case Now / Straight Case First | "Straight shooter" register. Give it to you straight. |
| 7 | **Fair Case** | Fair Case Now / Fair Case First | "Fair" is the claimant's own word. Already in Round 1 vocabulary. |
| 8 | **Clear Case** | Clear Case Now / Clear Case First | Alliterative. But "ClearCase" is an IBM software trademark — legal risk. |

**Ed McCabe — Craft Filter (cuts to 5):**

Square Case doesn't have the rhythm — 'square' works in 'square deal' because 'deal' is two syllables that balance it; 'case' is one and it doesn't land the same way.*

> *Now vs. First: 'Now' is more urgent and action-ready — it's the ad signoff. 'First' is more strategic — 'do this first, before anything else.' Both work for different names. Real Case Now is faster; Real Case First is smarter. Frank Case Now is immediately likeable; Frank Case First slightly stilted.*
>
> *Surviving: **Real Case Now** (both words upgraded from Round 1's Honest Claim), **True Case Now** (True was already strong; 'case' improves it), **Frank Case Now** (frank earns its keep — also the most distinctive word in the exercise), **Fair Case First** ('First' works best here: do this first, before you sign anything), **Level Case Now** (level is underused — the idiom is exactly right for this audience)."*

**Craft-surviving A:** Real Case Now, True Case Now, Frank Case Now, Fair Case First, Level Case Now

## SUB-DIRECTION B: Drop the Legal Noun — Everyday Language

*"What if there's no legal word in the name at all? Maria doesn't say 'claim.' She doesn't say 'case' either, half the time. She says 'the insurance thing.' She says 'this whole situation.' What does she understand in a half-second on a phone screen at 2am? 'Deal.' She knows what a deal is. 'Shot.' She knows what a fair shot is. 'Answer.' She knows what an honest answer is. This territory says: we're so confident in who we're talking to that we're not going to use any legal vocabulary at all."*

> *"Real Talk is slang that will date. Fair Say is British. Fair Stand is aggressive. Fair Know doesn't exist as a phrase. Fair Out is broken. >

**Craft-surviving B:** Fair Deal Now, Fair Shot Now, Real Answer Now, True Answer Now

## SUB-DIRECTION C: "First" as the Urgency Word — Priority, Not Pressure

**Jon Steel frames the territory:**

*"The case for 'First' over 'Now': 'Now' is temporal urgency — it's the adjuster's energy, honestly. 'Sign now, while the offer is on the table.' 'First' is temporal priority — it's the advisor's energy. 'Do this first, before anything else.' That's the distinction. 'First' implies the same timing without the pressure.*
>
*The ad structure with 'First': 'Before you say yes to anything — Real Case First.' It's a different emotional register. Less 'act before you miss out,' more 'the one thing you should do before you do anything else.' For Maria, who is already overwhelmed and being pressured by the adjuster, 'First' may actually be the more resonant word."*

**Ed McCabe — Raw Generation:**

*Note: Most "First" candidates share names with Sub-Direction A. This sub-direction focuses on names where "First" meaningfully changes the name, not just where it substitutes.*

| # | Name | Domain | Why "First" Changes It |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Fair Case First** | FairCaseFirst.com | "First" is the instruction: do this first, before you sign. Priority over pressure. |
| 2 | **Real Case First** | RealCaseFirst.com | Find out what your case really is, first — before the adjuster's offer becomes your only frame. |
| 3 | **True Case First** | TrueCaseFirst.com | The true case value — first. Before you're locked in. |
| 4 | **Fair Deal First** | FairDealFirst.com | Get a fair deal — first. Before you accept one that isn't. |
| 5 | **Know First** | KnowFirst.com | From Round 1. Find out what you have — first, before you decide anything. |
| 6 | **Ask First** | AskFirst.com | Ask before you sign. First call is free. The action is minimal. |
| 7 | **Case First** | CaseFirst.com | Your case — first. Before anything else. Minimal, possessive. |
| 8 | **Fair First** | FairFirst.com | Fair first — before you accept anything less. |
| 9 | **Real First** | RealFirst.com | The real picture — first. Slightly thin without a noun. |
| 10 | **Check First** | CheckFirst.com | Check what you have, first. Very action-oriented. |

**Ed McCabe — Craft Filter (cuts to 4):**

> *"Real First and Fair First are thin without a noun — the idea is there but the name doesn't land independently. Case First is interesting in its minimalism — it puts your case before anything else, literally — but it's too spare to carry a brand.*
>
> *Ask First is actually interesting and hasn't appeared before. 'Ask first' is the minimum viable action in two words that don't use any legal vocabulary. The implicit message: ask first, before you say yes to the first offer. Ask First Now and Ask First are both viable. Check First is similar — check before you sign.*
>
> *Surviving: **Fair Case First** (the priority frame for the fair-case territory), **Ask First** (new — minimum viable action with zero legal words), **Fair Deal First** (the non-legal urgency frame), **Know First** (re-evaluated — still strong in the 'First' register, information before commitment)."*

**Craft-surviving C:** Fair Case First, Ask First, Fair Deal First, Know First

## SUB-DIRECTION D: Single-Word Power Names + Now

**Jon Steel frames the territory:**

*"One word. Maximum meaning. 'Now' charges it. The discipline here is: the word has to work without the 'Now' in copy — the 'Now' can be in the URL and the ad signoff, but the brand itself has to stand on one word. This is the highest bar in the exercise, and only one word in the whole exercise is strong enough to clear it."*

**Ed McCabe — Raw Generation:**

| # | Name | + Now | Meaning Stack | Register Check |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Reclaim** | Reclaim Now | Re-claim: your income, your health, your normal life, the fair compensation the adjuster's offer is taking from you | No health register. No litigation register. The "claim" sound without "claim" as the visible word. |
| 2 | **Restore** | Restore Now | Get restored to where you were before | Health/wellness register — likely too adjacent |
| 3 | **Recover** | Recover Now | Financial and physical recovery | Health register — already used in medical contexts |
| 4 | **Rebuild** | Rebuild Now | Rebuild your life, your finances | Construction register, not legal or health |
| 5 | **Renew** | Renew Now | Start fresh, renewed | Insurance renewal register — wrong associations |
| 6 | **Reset** | Reset Now | Back to zero, start fresh | Tech register — also used in debt/financial reset contexts |
| 7 | **Rebalance** | Rebalance Now | Restore the balance — the insurance company tipped it, rebalance it | Financial/investment register |
| 8 | **Recoup** | Recoup Now | Recover what you lost. Financial and specific. | More financial/business register |
| 9 | **Resolve** | Resolve Now | Resolve the situation — but also resolution, resolution of the case | Slightly corporate, conflict-resolution register |
| 10 | **Recount** | Recount Now | Tell your story again, from the beginning — before the adjuster's version becomes the only one | Political register (election recounts) — wrong |

**Ed McCabe — Craft Filter (cuts to 2):**

> *"Restore and Recover are wellness brands. Renew is an insurance renewal product. Reset reads as a tech feature or debt-relief ad. Rebalance is a financial planning term. Recount has an election problem. Resolve is too generic — every conflict resolution service uses it.*
>
> *Two survive, and one is significantly stronger than the other.*
>
> ***Reclaim** is the word. Re-claim stacks every meaning this brand needs: reclaim your fair outcome, reclaim your income, reclaim the position you were in before someone else's negligence changed it. The 're-' prefix implies something was taken — which is true. It's not wellness. It's not litigation. 'Reclaim Now' is the cleanest single-word-plus-now construction in the exercise. ReclaimNow.com is almost certainly available.*
>
> ***Recoup** survives as a distant second — 'recoup what you lost' is specific and honest — but the word doesn't have the same emotional depth as 'Reclaim.' It's financial, not human.*
>
> *Surviving: **Reclaim Now**, **Recoup Now** (distant second, held for comparison)."*

**Craft-surviving D:** Reclaim Now, Recoup Now
