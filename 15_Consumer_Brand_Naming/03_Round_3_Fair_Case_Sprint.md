# B2C Consumer Brand — Round 3: Fair Case Sprint
*Second Chair / Session 15 — March 1, 2026*

---

## DAVIS'S DIRECTION ENTERING ROUND 3

**Shortlisted:** Fair Case Now (faircase.now) — the anchor. This is the territory.

**Cut:** True Case — sounds like it's protesting. "The true case" implies the others were false — overworking the honesty.

**Cut:** Fair Case First — "first" as the final word creates a grammatical oddity. "A fair case first?" doesn't land cleanly as a call to action.

**The question for Round 3:** If getting a fair case is the destination, can the brand name encode the idea that *this call is the first step toward it*? The brand IS the first move. The name should feel like: "start here, before anything else."

---

## THE 20 NAMES

Grouped by the approach each takes to the "first step" idea.

---

### GROUP 1: "Start" as the first-step word

"Start" carries the beginning without needing "first" or "step" as separate words. The call to action signs off with "Now."

| # | Name | Domain | The Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Fair Start Now** | FairStart.now | "Start here for a fair case." The first step is built into the word. "Fair Start Now" = this is where a fair outcome begins. |
| 2 | **Start Fair Now** | StartFair.now | Verb-first version. "Start fair" = begin from a fair position, before the adjuster sets the terms. |
| 3 | **Fair Case Start** | FairCaseStart.now | The most literal version: start your fair case. "Start" as the urgency word instead of "Now" — slightly slower but clear. |

---

### GROUP 2: "Step" and "Move" — encoding the first action

One step, one move, before anything else.

| # | Name | Domain | The Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | **Fair Step Now** | FairStep.now | One fair step before you decide. "Step" is more intimate than "move" — it's what you do first. |
| 5 | **Fair Move Now** | FairMove.now | "Make one fair move before you sign." Chess register but universally understood — a move you make before anything else. |
| 6 | **One Fair Step** | OneFairStep.now | The minimum viable action named exactly. One step. Fair. Before anything. |
| 7 | **One Fair Call** | OneFairCall.now | "Make one fair call before you decide." The action is the brand. |

---

### GROUP 3: "Path" — the route to a fair case

The brand is the beginning of a path, not a destination.

| # | Name | Domain | The Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8 | **Fair Path Now** | FairPath.now | "The path to a fair case starts here." Not a promise of the outcome — the beginning of the route. |
| 9 | **Fair Ground Now** | FairGround.now | "Even ground" + "a fair starting place." The ground you stand on before the adjuster sets the terms. "Fair Ground" also echoes "fairground" — familiar word, used differently. |

---

### GROUP 4: "Just" and "Only" — the fairness IS the first step

These say: fairness isn't the reward at the end of the process. It's what you're owed right now, at the very beginning.

| # | Name | Domain | The Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | **Just Fair Now** | JustFair.now | "Just" = rightful (a just outcome) + "only fair" (it's only fair you know what your case is worth). Two meanings, three words. "Just fair" is also what the claimant says: "I just want things to be fair." |
| 11 | **Only Fair Now** | OnlyFair.now | "It's only fair that you know what your case is worth before you say yes." The implicit argument for the first call, compressed into a brand name. |
| 12 | **Fair Enough Now** | FairEnough.now | "Fair enough" is an everyday idiom — agreement, reasonableness, "that sounds right." "Fair enough, now" = let's get you a fair outcome, now. |

---

### GROUP 5: "Your" — possession of the first step

"Your" makes the fair case theirs before they've done anything. The brand is already giving it to them.

| # | Name | Domain | The Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 13 | **Your Fair Case** | YourFairCase.now | Possessive + the whole idea. "Your fair case — now." The case belongs to them; this is how they get it. |
| 14 | **Your Fair Start** | YourFairStart.now | The starting point belongs to them. "Your fair start — before anything else." |
| 15 | **Your Fair Call** | YourFairCall.now | Double meaning: (1) it's your call to make — your decision, (2) make the fair call — call us. Both readings lead to the same action. |

---

### GROUP 6: The call to action as the brand name

No noun at all. The verb IS the brand.

| # | Name | Domain | The Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 16 | **Fair Call Now** | FairCallNow.com | "Make the fair call, now." The action and the name are the same thing. When someone calls, they've already completed the brand promise. |
| 17 | **Fair Ask Now** | FairAsk.now | "The fair ask" — asking fairly for what you're owed, before you sign anything. "Ask" is even lower commitment than "call." |
| 18 | **Get Fair Now** | GetFair.now | "Get fair treatment, now." The most direct version. Verb + adjective + urgency. |

---

### GROUP 7: "Open" — beginning the case

Opening a case is a legal metaphor that doesn't require legal vocabulary.

| # | Name | Domain | The Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 19 | **Fair Open Now** | FairOpen.now | "Open your case fairly, now." Opening = beginning, transparency, starting point. "Fair Open" also implies that the opening position is fair — unlike the insurance company's. |
| 20 | **Case Open Now** | CaseOpen.now | "Open your case — now, before you sign." "Case open" is the moment of beginning. The brand is the act of opening, not the outcome. |

---

## NIGEL'S READ

*"Twenty names, six approaches to the same idea: this is the first step toward a fair case.*

*The three I'd put in front of Davis immediately:*

***Fair Start Now** — 'start' carries the first-step meaning without any of the awkwardness of 'first.' It's a clean upgrade from 'Fair Case First.' Start is the action; Fair is the destination; Now is the instruction.*

***Just Fair Now** — the most interesting name in this round. 'Just' is doing three jobs: rightful, only/merely, and the colloquial 'I just want things to be fair.' It's also the word the claimant already uses — 'I just want to be fair about it.' That's a name that lives in her vocabulary.*

***Your Fair Call** — the double meaning is the strongest in the exercise. It's her call to make, and it's the call she should make. When she makes it, she's completed the brand promise without knowing it. No litigation. No pressure. Just: your call.*

*Fair Case Now stays on the table — it's still the cleanest. These twenty are ways to say the same thing differently, not replacements for it unless Davis finds one he prefers."*
